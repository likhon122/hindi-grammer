Tenses ( Identify Past tense in Hindi )

Sentence Structure

Subject + Ne (if transitive) + Verb Stem + ya/i/e/in

Example-

“Maine aam (mango) ka juice piya” or “I drank mango juice”

Tenses ( Identify Future tense in Hindi )

Sentence Structure

Subject + Verb Stem + nga/ngi/unge/ungi

Example-

“Vah cake khaega” Or “He will eat cake.”

“Ye usko vote denge” Or “They will vote him”

Each tense is further divided intodifferent types similar to English. For eg.Simple Future, Indefinite Future etc

Usage of Suffixes

"a," "i," & "e" in the past

In Hindi, verbs changed based on the gender and number of the subject in the past tense.

For masculine singular, use the prefix "a."

Example:

"Main school gaya" (I went to school) if the speaker is male ( मैं स्कूल गया। )

For feminine singular, use the suffix "i.“

Grammar Review

English Hindi Term Extra notes
Noun Sangya Yah kitaab meri hai. This book is mine.
Pronoun Sarvanaam Woh mera dost hai. He is my friend.
Verb Kriya Main likh raha hoon. I am writing.
Adjective Visheshan Woh sundar ladki hai. She is a beautiful girl.
Adverb Kriya Visheshan Woh jaldi daudta hai. He runs quickly.
Proposition Sambandh Bodhak Main ghar mein hoon. I am in the house.
Conjunction Sanyojak Main aur tum dost hain. You and I are friends.
Interjection Vismayadi Bodhak Waah Tumne accha kaam kiya. Wow! You did a great job.
Articles Nirdeshak Yah ek kursi hai. This is a chair.
Subject-Verb Agreement Kartaa-Kriya Samvidhaan Woh padhta hai vs Woh padhti hai. He reads vs She reads.

Adjectives

Qualitative Adjectives (Gunvachak Visheshan)

In Hindi, verbs changed based on the gender and number of the subject in the past tense.

Quantitative Adjectives (Parimaanvachak Visheshan)

Indicate the amount or quantity of a noun, specifyinghow much or how many.

Demonstrative Adjectives (Nishchayvachak Visheshan)

Point out or specify a particular noun, showing itsposition or relation.

Possessive Adjectives (Swaamitvachak Visheshan)

Show ownership or possession related to a noun.
